Welcome to Our Services Page! Here at The Leopard Times, Our goal is to educate people about leopards (Panthera pardus) and inspire wildlife conservation. Although leopards are highly diverse, their populations are threatened and steadily declining, primarily due to habitat loss and poaching. They are listed as vulnurable by the IUCN, with certain subspecies currently more endangered than others. In fact, out of the 9 subspecies of leopards, 5 are listed as endangered or critically endangered.

Major Threats

Habitat Loss: In Asia, 84% of original habitat has vanished

Prey Depletion: As habitats deplete, so do the species that make up a leopard's diet

Illegal wildlife Trade: Thousands of animals fall victim to the trade for their fur, bones, and teeth. Cubs are often smuggled and sold as exotic pets

Human-Wildlife Conflict: As humans begin to settle closer to leopard territories, both are forced to share the landscapes, resulting into more frequent run-ins that can be fatal. Leopards will also sometimes prey on livestock, leading to retaliatory killing by farmers.

What We Are Doing to Help

The Leopard Times works to educate the public about these amazing big cats. Our leopard zoo tours have become popular among children and adults alike. Together we travel to zoos around the United States to see leopards up close and personal. We also have yearly trips to Africa and Asia to see these magnificant animals in their natural habitat.
